Overview

The Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) is a premier mixed martial arts (MMA) organization globally, known for hosting some of the most high-profile and competitive bouts in combat sport. Founded in 1993, the UFC is headquartered in Las Vegas, Nevada, and has played a significant role in popularizing MMA worldwide

History

Established in 1993 by Art Davie and Rorion Gracie, among others, to identify the most effective martial art in a real fight. Initially, there were no weight classes, minimal rules, and fights ended only by knockout, submission, or the corner throwing in the towel. Over time, the UFC introduced weight classes, rounds, and a standardized set of rules to ensure fighter safety and gain mainstream acceptance.

Rules

The UFC follows the Unified Rules of MMA, with fights typically consisting of three or five five-minute rounds. Permitted techniques include striking and grappling, while illegal actions encompass strikes to the back of the head, eye gouging, and groin attacks. Fights can end via knockout, submission, decision, or disqualification.

Divsions

The UFC has multiple weight classes for both men and women, each with its own championship title:

  1. Flyweight: Up to 125 lbs (56.7 kg)
  2. Bantamweight: Up to 135 lbs (61.2 kg)
  3. Featherweight: Up to 145 lbs (65.8 kg)
  4. Lightweight: Up to 155 lbs (70.3 kg)
  5. Welterweight: Up to 170 lbs (77.1 kg)
  6. Middleweight: Up to 185 lbs (83.9 kg)
  7. Light Heavyweight: Up to 205 lbs (93 kg)
  8. Heavyweight: 205 to 265 lbs (93 to 120.2 kg)
